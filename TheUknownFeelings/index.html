By Kinga Nowak

About the exhibition

The purpose of this exhibition is to present the power of the words we use every day. Through words, we understand our world, we communicate with other people, we can express our opinion, identify and record our memories and experiences.

Words have the power to shape, create things and stories. According to modern linguists, there are about 7,000 languages, not including dialects. Each of them has different words and an idea to describe the same thing. However, there are exceptions to which this work is focused. In every language, there are words that cannot be translated into another language. This is because a country's culture is different from others. For the needs of certain events, situations or emotions, words are created that enable the inhabitants of a given country to communicate and express their feelings better and easier.

Most of the humanity lives in the world without realizing that certain feelings and situations have a name. Ignorance of the existence of these words causes people to lose the necessary parts of existence that could have changed the course of events in their lives. Untranslatable words have a force that changes, strengthens, and brings meaning and benefits to human life.

About the author

Kinga Nowak

Kinga Nowak is a third-year student of Art History and Museum Studies at Liverpool John Moores University. Her interest in art began in 2008 when she joined the Art Studio Creation, which allowed Kinga to learn new painting and graphic techniques, knowledge about art, and numerous trips around the world, which included visiting the most important museums and places of art. Kinga is the laureate of the 19th Kanagawa Biennial World’s Art Exhibition and the winner of the "Primus" title for outstanding cultural and artistic achievements in 2017 in Rybnik. Kinga was a juror of the 15th International Art Competition ‘The Great Well-Known and Unknown Artists - William Turner – Sun in the Fog’. Since 2014 Kinga participated in 3 international Erasmus+ exchanges in Italy, Ireland, and Poland, which consisted in getting to know young people from different countries and jointly creating artistic, sports, and cultural projects. To gain experience working in culture, Kinga completed an internship at the Gallery of Contemporary Art in Katowice and an internship at the Cinematography Foundation in Warsaw. In 2020, Kinga started to create her own ceramics and found a direction in which she would like to develop. She aims to be an art and pottery teacher.
